The Fascinating Red Panda

The red panda, often called the "lesser panda," is a small mammal native to the eastern Himalayas and southwestern China. Despite its name, it is not closely related to the giant panda. It has reddish-brown fur, a long, shaggy tail, and a waddling gait due to its shorter front legs.

Quick Facts

  • Scientific Name: Ailurus fulgens
  • Diet: Primarily bamboo, but also eats eggs, birds, and insects.
  • Special Feature: They have a "false thumb," which is an extension of the wrist bone, to help them grip bamboo.
  • Conservation Status: Endangered, with fewer than 10,000 mature individuals estimated to be in the wild.
