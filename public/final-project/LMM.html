Different Types of Generative AI

Generative AI includes Language Models (LM), Large Language Models (LLM), and GPT. Here's a quick breakdown:

Language Model (LM)

A model of the human brain's ability to produce natural language.

Large Language Model (LLM)

A language model trained with self-supervised machine learning on billions of words. Used for complex tasks like chatbots and content creation.

Generative Pre-Trained Transformer (GPT)

A type of LLM widely used in chatbots. Trained on large datasets of unlabeled content. Great for writing, coding, and conversation.

Diffusion-Based Image Generators (DBIG)

  • T2I: Text to image — converts text into images.
  • T2V: Text to video — creates short video sequences from text.

Summary Table

  • Language Model (LM) – Understands and generates human-like text. Used for chatbots, summarization, translation.
  • Large Language Model (LLM) – More advanced version of LM. Trained on billions of words. Used for personal assistants, content creation.
  • GPT – Type of LLM common in chatbots. Great for writing, coding, conversation.
  • T2I / T2V – Diffusion-based models for images and videos. Used for art, animation, storytelling.
